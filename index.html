Products & Plans
Every product line Integrity sells — how each works, who it is for, and what differentiates them. Know these cold before Monday.
Medicare Advantage
Part C · HMO / PPO / SNP
Primary Product
A private insurance plan that replaces Original Medicare. Must cover everything Original Medicare covers, but often includes dental, vision, hearing, gym, transportation, and drug coverage bundled in — often at $0 monthly premium.
Key Features
  • Often $0 monthly premium
  • Extra benefits (dental, vision, hearing, gym)
  • Usually includes Part D drug coverage
  • Annual Maximum Out-of-Pocket (MOOP) cap
  • Plans change annually — review recommended
Things to Consider with Clients
  • Are doctors/hospitals in-network?
  • Are medications on the formulary?
  • Is there a deductible (and on which tiers)?
  • Does the client travel across state lines?
  • Qualifies for Low Income Subsidy or Medicaid?
HMO: Strict network, lowest cost PPO: Flexible network, moderate cost SNP: Special Needs Plan for dual-eligibles & chronic conditions
Medicare Supplement (Medigap)
Plans A–N · Issued by private carriers
Core Product
Works alongside Original Medicare (does not replace it). Pays for gaps Medicare doesn't cover — copays, deductibles, coinsurance. Client shows two cards at doctor's office. Accepted anywhere Medicare is accepted in the US — no network restrictions.
Most Popular Plans at Integrity
  • Plan G — Most comprehensive for new enrollees (Part B deductible not covered)
  • Plan N — Lower premium with small copays for office/ER visits
  • Plan F — No longer for new enrollees after Jan 1, 2020 (existing holders keep it)
  • Premiums typically $100–$300/month
Key Differences vs. Medicare Advantage
  • No network restrictions — nationwide coverage
  • No referrals needed for specialists
  • Ideal for frequent travelers
  • Does NOT include dental, vision, hearing
  • Requires separate Part D plan for drugs
Part D — Prescription Drug Plan
Standalone PDP · Add-on for Supplement clients
Add-On
Required for clients on Original Medicare + Supplement. Covers prescription drugs. Formularies (covered drug lists) and cost structures vary significantly by carrier. Clients who don't enroll when eligible face a late enrollment penalty.
  • Can be purchased as standalone plan alongside Original Medicare or Supplement
  • Most Medicare Advantage plans bundle Part D — no separate plan needed
  • Low Income Subsidy (LIS/Extra Help) can reduce or eliminate premiums for qualifying clients
  • Plans change annually — review formulary & cost structure each Open Enrollment

Compliance & Regulations
The regulatory framework governing Integrity Insurance — federal CMS rules, Colorado state law, and what compliance means for Chris's day-to-day work.
Why this matters for Chris: Even as an administrative support role, Chris will handle SOA forms, client records, and marketing materials. Federal CMS Rules — Critical for Admin Staff
Rule Requirement Admin Impact
SOA — 48-Hour Rule Scope of Appointment must be collected at least 48 hours before a Medicare sales appointment. Exceptions: walk-ins, inbound calls, or within 4 days of enrollment period end. Chris must track SOA dates and flag any scheduled appointments that haven't reached the 48-hour window.
SOA — Retention SOA forms must be retained for a minimum of 10 years, even if no sale was made. All SOAs must be filed in the CRM immediately after collection. Never discard.
SOA — Scope Limits Agents may only discuss the plan types listed on the SOA. If the client wants to discuss something not listed, a new SOA must be completed and a new appointment scheduled (48-hr wait). Understand this so Chris can brief clients correctly when scheduling.
TPMO Disclosure All marketing materials must include the CMS TPMO disclaimer: "We do not offer every plan available in your area. Currently we represent 9 organizations which offer 133 products in your area..." Chris must verify this disclosure is present on any marketing material before distribution.
Marketing Dates Agents cannot market next year's plans until October 1. Cannot enroll until October 15. No promotional materials for upcoming plan year before Oct 1.
Call Recording All sales, marketing, and enrollment calls must be recorded in their entirety. Ensure recording systems are active and functional for every client interaction.
Educational vs. Sales Events Cannot hold a sales/marketing event within 12 hours of an educational event at the same or adjacent location. Cannot collect SOA cards at educational events. Event scheduling must respect this separation.
CMS Pre-Enrollment Checklist Before enrollment, agents must fully discuss: network providers, pharmacy network, drug formulary, costs, premiums, benefits, and specific health care needs. Intake checklist must capture this information before appointment handoff.
Colorado State Regulations (DORA — Division of Insurance)
RequirementDetail
License Required Colorado Life & Health (Accident & Health) license required to sell Medicare Advantage, Supplement, Part D, or Life products. Issued by DORA DOI.
Pre-Licensing Education Typically 40–52 hours of pre-licensing coursework before sitting for the exam. Kaplan, ExamFX, and similar providers offer Colorado-approved courses.
Licensing Exam Pearson VUE exam — approximately 150 questions, 70% passing score required. Fee: ~$47.
License Application Filed through NIPR or Sircon within 12 months of passing exam. Fee: $47 + $5.60 NIPR transaction fee.
Renewal Cycle Every 2 years, on the licensee's birthday month.
Continuing Education 24 CE hours per 2-year cycle: 18 hours in line of authority (Life/Health), 3 hours ethics, 3 hours elective. No grace period — must be complete before renewal.
AHIP Certification Required annually to sell Medicare Advantage products. America's Health Insurance Plans exam — ~$175/year. Must be completed before AEP (typically by Sept).
Carrier Appointments Licensed agents must also be appointed by each carrier they represent. Integrity's FMO (Impact Legacy Group) assists with contracting.
HIPAA Client PHI (Protected Health Information) must be handled per HIPAA. No sharing over unsecured channels. Proper document disposal required.

Module 5
Key Terminology
The language of Medicare insurance. Use these terms naturally in the interview to demonstrate preparation and industry literacy.
Amanda has 20+ years in this industry. Using correct terminology signals that Chris has taken the time to prepare — exactly the quality she is looking for in a Client Delight Specialist.
Medicare Parts A & B
Original Medicare. Part A = hospital coverage (inpatient). Part B = medical coverage (outpatient, doctors). Both have deductibles and cost-sharing.
Medicare Advantage (Part C)
Private plan that replaces Original Medicare. Must cover A+B benefits. Often includes Part D, dental, vision, hearing. Integrity's primary product.
Medigap / Medicare Supplement
Secondary insurance that fills cost gaps in Original Medicare. Pays after Medicare pays. No networks — accepted anywhere Medicare is accepted.
Part D — Prescription Drug Plan
Standalone or bundled drug coverage. Each plan has a formulary (drug list). Low Income Subsidy (LIS/Extra Help) available for qualifying clients.
SOA — Scope of Appointment
Federally required CMS form signed before any Medicare sales meeting. Specifies exactly which plan types will be discussed. Must be collected 48 hours in advance (with exceptions). Retained 10 years.
AEP — Annual Enrollment Period
Oct 15 – Dec 7. The primary window when any Medicare beneficiary can switch plans. The busiest period for the agency.
IEP — Initial Enrollment Period
7-month window (3 months before, month of, 3 months after 65th birthday) when a person first becomes eligible for Medicare.
SEP — Special Enrollment Period
Time-limited enrollment window triggered by qualifying life events: losing employer coverage, moving, gaining Medicaid, etc.
Dual Eligible
A person who qualifies for both Medicare (federal, age 65+) and Medicaid (state, low income). Integrity has specific expertise here. Key link to Complex Home Care Solutions' client population.
SNP — Special Needs Plan
Medicare Advantage plan with enhanced benefits for dual-eligibles (D-SNP), those in institutions (I-SNP), or those with chronic conditions (C-SNP). Offers grocery cards, OTC credits, transportation, and more.
MOOP — Maximum Out-of-Pocket
The cap on what a member pays in a plan year. Once reached, the plan covers 100% of covered services. Critical concept for comparing Medicare Advantage plans.
Formulary
The list of prescription drugs covered by a Part D or Medicare Advantage plan. Organized by tiers that determine copay amounts. Must be verified for each client's medications.
LIS / Extra Help
Federal Low Income Subsidy program that reduces or eliminates Part D premiums and copays for qualifying low-income beneficiaries. Often relevant for dual-eligible clients.
FMO — Field Marketing Organization
Distributor between carriers and independent agents. Integrity works through Impact Legacy Group as its FMO. Provides CRM, compliance support, quoting tools, and contracting assistance.
TPMO — Third-Party Marketing Org
CMS designation for independent agents and brokers who market Medicare plans. All TPMOs must include CMS-required disclaimers in marketing materials.
AHIP Certification
Annual America's Health Insurance Plans certification required to sell Medicare Advantage. Must be completed before the AEP marketing period begins. Cost ~$175/year.
SunFire Matrix
Integrity's primary quoting and enrollment platform. Allows agents to compare Medicare plans across carriers, generate Summary of Benefits, and submit enrollments electronically.
Summary of Benefits (SOB)
Standardized document that outlines a plan's coverage, costs, and rules. Must be provided to clients before enrollment. Generated through SunFire or directly from the carrier.
HMO vs. PPO (MA subtypes)
HMO: Must use in-network providers (except emergencies). Lower cost, more restrictions. PPO: Can use out-of-network providers at higher cost. More flexibility. Key factor in doctor network questions.
DORA DOI
Colorado Department of Regulatory Agencies Division of Insurance. Issues and regulates insurance licenses in Colorado. Sircon and NIPR are the portals used to apply and renew.
